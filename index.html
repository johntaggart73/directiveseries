The Series

The Directive Series follows Aimee Mercer — Pulitzer-winning journalist, defense budget specialist, and the woman who finds an anomaly that shouldn't exist. Four books. A single thread from a line in a spreadsheet to a tower built on something no one in the room understands. She exposes the largest environmental cover-up in history without ever learning what she was really standing over.

The Architecture

The Directive Series runs parallel to The Protocol Series and The Sequence Series. Three investigations. Three protagonists who never meet. The reader holds all three simultaneously and watches each character approach the same truth from a completely different angle — and never quite reach it.

The Wardenclyffe Directive

The budget anomaly was a rounding error. Until it wasn't.

Aimee Mercer has spent fifteen years following government money to places it wasn't supposed to go. A three-hundred-million-dollar defense line item coded to an Antarctic research station nobody can find leads her from a Senate subcommittee hearing to the ice shelf — and to a structure that doesn't appear on any satellite image taken after 2019. The cover-up is real. The people maintaining it are serious. What the tower was built to do is the question Aimee never quite reaches the bottom of.

The Protagonist

Aimee Mercer

Investigative Journalist · Pulitzer Prize, 2021

Aimee Mercer is the best defense budget journalist in the country, which is a specialty so narrow that most people have never heard of it. She won her Pulitzer for a series on procurement fraud that sent three contractors to prison and closed a program that had been running for eleven years. She is forty-one years old, works out of a sublet in Savannah, and does not own a television. She follows money. She has always followed money. The Wardenclyffe budget line is the strangest thing she has ever found — because the money is real, the station exists, and no one who should know about it does.

ExpertiseDefense procurement, budget forensics, source cultivation, FOIA litigation
MethodFollow the money. Always the money. Everything else is interpretation.
